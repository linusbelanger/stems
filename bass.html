BASS

Bass is both a frequncy range (along with mids and treble) as well as a type of instrument. Technically speaking, bass is any sound predominantly in 60-250 Hz range and is used to fill out the lower register of a song, often following the root notes of a chord procression.

Bass can come in the form of real instruments (bass guitar, tuba, etc.), digital/electronic instruments (sub, synth, 808, etc.), or even percussion (kick drum).

TOOLS

Below is a collection of free bass tools that I personally use on a regular basis.


KEE BASS

Martinic's VST emulation of the Rheem Kee Bass, a monophonic string bass simulator.

 BASS P LITE

Ample's VST emulation of the classic Fender Precision Bass guitar.

 '60s P BASS

IK Multimedia's VST emulation of a '60s Fender Presicion Bass guitar.
BASSLANE

A bass mixing plugin from Tone Project to add stereo harmony to your lower frequencies.

 SOUND ON SOUND

From Sound on Sound: Mixing Bass: How To Craft The Perfect Bottom End.

 MASTERING.COM

A YouTube course from Mastering.com titled "The Bass Guitar Mixing Trick for Clean Low End."
